This document presents the complete mathematical model for analyzing a beta-type Stirling engine, showing how initial geometric and thermodynamic parameters are transformed through fundamental physics equations to produce the characteristic P-V diagram.

Note: The beta-type configuration has both pistons in the same cylinder. The power piston controls total volume while the displacer redistributes gas between hot and cold spaces.

2. Initial Parameters

2.1 Geometric Parameters

Parameter Symbol Value Unit

Cylinder area A π(D/2)² = 1.963×10⁻³ m²
Power piston crank radius rp 0.025 m
Power piston connecting rod lp 0.075 m
Displacer crank radius rd 0.020 m
Displacer connecting rod ld 0.140 m

Parameter Symbol Value Unit

Vswept,p = A × 2rp = 1.963×10⁻³ × 0.050 = 9.817×10⁻⁵ m³
Vdead = Vswept,p/(CR - 1) = 9.817×10⁻⁵/0.7 = 1.402×10⁻⁴ m³

3. Volume Calculations - Crank-Slider Kinematics

3.1 Crank-Slider Position Equations

The exact crank-slider equations account for the finite connecting rod length and provide accurate piston positions:

Power Piston Position

βp(θ) = arcsin(rp × sin(θ) / lp)
xp,crank(θ) = rp × cos(θ) + lp × cos(βp)
xp(θ) = (rp + lp) - xp,crank(θ)
Where:
• θ = crank angle (radians)
• βp = connecting rod angle
• xp = piston displacement from TDC

Displacer Position (with Phase Shift)

θd = θ - φ
βd(θ) = arcsin(rd × sin(θd) / ld)
xd,crank(θ) = rd × cos(θd) + ld × cos(βd)
xd(θ) = (rd + ld) - xd,crank(θ)
Key Point: The displacer leads the power piston by phase angle φ (typically 90°).

3.2 Beta-Type Engine Volume Model

Critical Concept: In a beta-type engine, the total volume depends ONLY on the power piston position. The displacer merely redistributes the working gas between hot and cold spaces.

Total Volume

Vtotal(θ) = Vdead,total + A × xp(θ)

Volume Distribution

The displacer position determines how the working volume is split between compression and expansion spaces:

xd,normalized = xd(θ) / xd,max
fsplit = 0.5 × (1 - cos(π × xd,normalized))
Vworking = Vtotal - Vdead,total
Vcompression(θ) = Vdead,cold + Vworking × (1 - fsplit)
Vexpansion(θ) = Vdead,hot + Vworking × fsplit

Volume Conservation Check

Vtotal = Vcompression + Vexpansion + Vregenerator

4. Schmidt Analysis - Pressure Calculations

4.1 Schmidt Theory Assumptions

1. Isothermal spaces: Temperature constant in each space
2. Uniform pressure: Pressure equal throughout engine at any instant
3. Ideal regenerator: Perfect heat transfer effectiveness
4. Mass conservation: Total mass of working fluid remains constant

4.2 Total Mass Calculation

The total mass is determined from known conditions at Bottom Dead Center (BDC):

[~, bdc_idx] = max(Vtotal)   // Find BDC index

At BDC, with known pressure PBDC = 500 kPa:

PBDC = (mtotal × R) / (Vcomp,BDC/Tc + Vreg/Tr + Vexp,BDC/Th)

Solving for total mass:

mtotal = PBDC × (Vcomp,BDC/Tc + Vreg/Tr + Vexp,BDC/Th) / R

4.3 Instantaneous Pressure - The Schmidt Equation

Fundamental Schmidt Equation: This is the core equation that relates pressure to volume distribution and temperatures throughout the cycle.
P(θ) = (mtotal × R) / (Vcomp(θ)/Tc + Vreg/Tr + Vexp(θ)/Th)

4.4 Mass Distribution Verification

For validation, the mass in each space can be calculated:

mcomp(θ) = P(θ) × Vcomp(θ) / (R × Tc)
mexp(θ) = P(θ) × Vexp(θ) / (R × Th)
mreg = P(θ) × Vreg / (R × Tr)
mtotal = mcomp + mexp + mreg = constant ✓

5. Work and Power Calculations

5.1 Method 1: Direct Integration (Indicated Work)

The work per cycle is the integral of pressure over volume change:

Windicated = ∮ P dV = ∫02π P(θ) × (dV/dθ) dθ

Numerically computed using trapezoidal integration:

Windicated = trapz(Vtotal, P)
Sign Convention: Positive work = clockwise P-V cycle = power production
Negative work = counter-clockwise cycle = power consumption

5.2 Method 2: Mean Effective Pressure (MEP)

Vswept = max(Vtotal) - min(Vtotal)
MEP = |Windicated| / Vswept
WMEP = MEP × Vswept

Both methods should agree within 5% for validation.

5.3 Power Output

Pindicated = Windicated × foperating
PMEP = WMEP × foperating
Where: foperating = operating frequency (Hz) = RPM/60

5.4 Thermal Efficiency

Heat input for isothermal expansion:

Qin = mtotal × R × Th × ln(Vmax/Vmin)

Thermal efficiency:

η = Windicated / Qin

Carnot efficiency limit:

ηCarnot = 1 - Tc/Th = 1 - 300/900 = 0.667
Validation: η must be less than ηCarnot (Second Law of Thermodynamics)

6. Torque Analysis

6.1 Power Piston Torque

Force on power piston from gas pressure:

Fpower(θ) = (P(θ) - Patm) × A

Torque using crank-slider mechanics:

Tpower(θ) = Fpower × rp × sin(θ) / cos(βp)

6.2 Total Engine Torque

For beta-type engines with negligible displacer rod diameter:

Ttotal(θ) = Tpower(θ)

Mean torque:

Tmean = (1/2π) × ∫02π Ttotal(θ) dθ

6.3 Torque Characteristics

• Peak torques typically 5-10× mean torque
• 2-4 zero crossings per cycle
• Oscillating pattern requires flywheel for smooth operation

7. P-V Diagram Generation

7.1 Creating the P-V Diagram

The P-V diagram is generated by plotting pressure P(θ) versus total volume Vtotal(θ) for a complete cycle:

θ = [0, 2π] with 360 points
Plot: P(θ) vs Vtotal(θ)

7.2 Key Features of the P-V Diagram

Clockwise Cycle: Indicates positive work output (power production)
Area Enclosed: Represents work per cycle Windicated
Shape: Quasi-elliptical due to finite heat transfer rates

7.3 Ideal vs Real Cycle Comparison

Aspect Ideal Stirling Cycle Real (Schmidt) Cycle
Compression Isothermal at Tc Quasi-isothermal
Heat Addition Isochoric (constant volume) Continuous during expansion
Expansion Isothermal at Th Quasi-isothermal
Heat Rejection Isochoric (constant volume) Continuous during compression
P-V Shape Rectangular with curved isotherms Smooth elliptical

Physical Insights

1. Phase Relationship: Displacer leads power piston to ensure proper gas shuttling
2. Temperature Ratio: Higher Th/Tc increases efficiency and power
3. Dead Volume: Reduces compression ratio and efficiency
4. Regenerator: Critical for achieving high efficiency
5. Flywheel: Essential for smooth operation due to torque fluctuation
